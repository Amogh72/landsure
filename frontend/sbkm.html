Our Aim

LandSure, created by five students from VIT Pune, aims to revolutionize how land transactions take place in India. The project’s mission is to make property deals transparent, secure, and fraud-free by combining verified government data, blockchain-based ownership records, and AI-powered document verification. With a focus on solving real-world challenges like forged papers, unclear titles, and ownership disputes, LandSure envisions a future where every land transaction is digitally verifiable, trusted, and accessible to everyone.

Blockchain Integration
LandSure uses the Ethereum blockchain to store verified property records immutably. Each transaction is secured with smart contracts, ensuring transparent ownership transfer and eliminating tampering or unauthorized modifications.
Smart Contracts
Smart contracts automate agreement enforcement between buyers and sellers. They execute property transfers only when all predefined conditions — such as payment completion and document validation — are met.
OCR and AI Verification
The platform employs Optical Character Recognition (OCR) combined with AI to extract and verify details from scanned property documents. This helps detect forged or duplicate entries and ensures all uploaded files match official records.
Tokenization
Each verified land parcel is converted into a digital token based on Ethereum’s ERC-721 standard. These tokens represent ownership rights and enable secure digital trading, fractional ownership, and transfer traceability.
Data Security & Privacy
Sensitive user and property data are encrypted before storage. The blockchain ledger preserves integrity, while access control ensures that only verified parties can view or modify specific land details.
Scalability & Integration
LandSure’s modular backend, built using Node.js and MongoDB, ensures scalability and fast data retrieval. It can integrate with government APIs and other real estate systems to expand verification coverage nationwide.
Agricultural Land

The agricultural land sector is seeing renewed interest as investors diversify beyond urban real estate. With the rise of agritech and government initiatives supporting digital land records, farmland prices in semi-urban areas have increased by 8–12% annually in the last few years. Verified ownership through blockchain can further attract institutional buyers and reduce land disputes.

Residential Land

Residential land demand continues to surge, particularly in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities, driven by remote work trends and affordable housing schemes. Property transparency and instant verification through platforms like LandSure can reduce buyer hesitation and speed up transactions, improving liquidity in this segment.

Commercial Land

Commercial plots in industrial corridors and logistic hubs are witnessing significant value appreciation due to infrastructure projects and manufacturing growth. Tokenization of such lands can make fractional ownership viable — enabling small investors to participate in high-value properties securely through blockchain-backed proof.
